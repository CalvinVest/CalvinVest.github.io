I'm a Computer Science major.

I've been a student at Western Governor's University since 2021, earning my BSCS.

I've moved schools a couple times.

I first started college courses in the fall of 2014, as a dual-credit high school student. I started as an Engineering major. Since then, I've transferred to Southern Illinois University as an Electrical Engineering, than an Electrical and Computer Engineering student. Around the time of the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, I chose to change direction, and start pursuing a degree in computer science. I changed my major and transferred to Western Governor's University, and I've been there since.

Schooling History

  • Illinois Central College

    Attended from August 2014 to December 2017

    At ICC, I started as a dual-credit high school student and completed courses for the Associates of Engineering degree. I completed projects in a variety of engineering disciplines, including computer-aided design (CAD), software development, and electrical engineering and circuit design.

  • Southern Illinois University - Edwardsville

    Attended from January 2018 to December 2020

    At SIUE, I specialized my degree program to Electrical Engineering, and later specialized further to Electrical and Computer Engineering. I completed more program-relevant courses, including advanced circuit analysis and electromagnetism.

  • Western Governors University

    Attending from August 2021 to present

    Leading into the 2020 Covid-19 pandemic, I realized that I wanted to change my degree. I transferred my academic progress and began taking online, asynchronous classes with WGU. I began my progress in the university's BS in Computer Science program as a fully-remote student. I've pretty well completed all of my general education courses by this point, so much of my coursework with WGU has been specialized technical content, including advanced software development, networking, project management, and program-relevant courses like discrete mathematics and source control.
