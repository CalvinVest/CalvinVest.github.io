About this website

What is this?

This website is, simply, me. It's a whole lot of information about my hobbies, interests, achievements, research, records, and so much more all in one place.

The tabs along the top of the page will be your best navigator here. Everything's accessible from most of the site, but some of the specific subsections might have specialized headers for their own categories. I put MAD amounts of time into getting everything on this site laid out in a way that, at least to me, makes sense. CRAZY amounts time. RIDICULOUS even.

What's the point?

It's ease of access. All of the stuff I could want to look at, all in a place that I can host from anywhere, in a format that's super easy to adventure through. Call it my information-age attempt at a wizard tower far away where I can store all of my spells and incantations and fancy wizard stuff when I wanna look at it for reasons and stuff.

Why a website, then?

So I can share it with you! And a bunch of other people, really. Information's nothing if it's not available. I want to share what I've learned with the people I want to teach it to, in a way that makes learning it easy. At its core, that's why this website's here. To share what I've learned about the world with the other people I'm sharing it with, to make it a little better.

My Philosophy

The cycle of knowledge as I've come to understand it has five parts:

  • Unknowing
  • Exploration
  • Research
  • Trial
  • Understanding

Every knowable thing begins in some form of the unknown. By exploring our surroundings, we gather valuable intelligence about the world that can create our context of it. Research requires us to compare the intelligence we've collected amongst an array of prior context. As new information is assessed, it expands the context that we ascertain about the world around us and its laws and principles. By comparing all new knowledge against this intellectual frame of reference composed of works across many different fields, experiences, specialties, and qualifications, we can further refine our own understanding not just of the unfamiliar, but of that which is known already also. This trial by which all information is processed and confirmed allows for a broader understanding of its subject and justifications for the validity of the data, its authorship, its authenticity, and its value.

Annomor

Hehe, this is where it gets a little cheesy but this one's my own thing.

Annomor is an acronymic portmanteau of the latin motto "ANtiquis reverentium, NOtitiae custodium, aMORi devotionem". To the ancients, reverence. To knowledge, guardianship. To love, devotion.

Antiquis reverentium, or "to the ancients, reverence", highlights my appreciation and interest in genealogy and religious history, as cataloged and referenced within this site.

Notitiae custodium, "to knowledge, guardianship", is my belief that human insight and wisdom is to be procured, deliberated, refined, vetted, sourced, and cited to the best of the ability of its stewards, so that it might serve to better the future through our inspection of the past.

Amori devotionem, "to love, devotion", encapsulates the moral responsibility of those with knowledge to share it, in love, for the betterment of another. In all knowledge and wisdom there is value, and in sharing this treasure with our neighbor and through good and proper stewardship of this knowledge might we show our love for those we learn for. To better the lives of those around us with all that we know and can learn, to leave this place better than we once knew it so that it is only made better for your time in it.
