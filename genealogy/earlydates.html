Where do I come from?

It's basically the foundational question of genealogy

I've spent a lot of time researching countless different lines of my family tree and searching for the answer to "what are my roots?" Every ancestor has their own rich story to tell, of immigration, armed service, stories in the newspaper, a tale of relocation throughout the American Midwest over the course of several census records. All of these sources and more allow me to view further and further back into my family history. I'll break down several important branches of my family tree below to go into more detail on the progress I've made in tracing my ancestry.

The Paternal Line

My Vest-Scranton paternal family history is primarily Northwestern and Germanic European. My paternal DNA's ancestral regions can be similarly divided into ⅓ Germanic, ⅓ English, and ⅓ a mix of Scottish, Dutch, Welsh, and others.

My paternal tree can be divided into its own branches.
