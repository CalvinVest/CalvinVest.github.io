  • The Data

    When I spend some time working on a family tree, I like to update this record. It's my way of making sure I stay on track by keeping my research complete and fresh.

    I use some pretty simple metrics, and use data taken directly from my Ancestry trees for the respectively titled families. The data is comprised of the total number of people within a tree, and the total number of records attached to all members of that tree. The ratio of records per person within the tree is my metric for determining the "research depth" of a tree. The more records per person, the more likely the tree is to be well-researched and fleshed out in a meaningful way.

    I did this because the old family trees I used to make were chock full of mistakes. Incorrect relationships, mispelled names, some blatantly wrong assumptions, and general negligence for proper formatting have made my old genealogy work almost beyond saving.
